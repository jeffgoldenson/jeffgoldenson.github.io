What’s an institution

Institutions are the humanly devised constraints that structure political, economic and social interaction.

Throughout history, institutions have been devised by human beings to create order and reduce uncertainty in exchange.
					
_Institutions, Douglass C. North, Journal of Economic Perspectives, 1991_

Institutions don’t like Institutions don’t like risk, as they shouldn’t. The Public Library is the same as when you were a kid, that’s what makes them wonderful. They have earned you trust by staying the same. Institutions are not designed to change. They are designed to earn your trust.

Colleges are similar. They are accredited entities, legally authorized to exchange a degree for measurable mastery. If that gets compromised, we’re all out of a job. And that’s why they have Labs.

Show Business is in the business of taking risks on ideas. Let’s take some cues from them…
