How to “Graduate” in 26 Hours

When we came together Wednesday, March 11th, 9:15 for final get together before Spring Break, everything was different.

Everyone at the table

Welcome to the Greenhouse🌱.

We’re a mostly no-credit, non-departmental creative studio inside Olin College. We grow young ideas, not plants.

We’re a crosscutting bunch - staff, faculty and students alike - sitting around a table making time for 🧠storming together. We’re present for each other as people. At best we come together as a production company. We produce culture that builds our community.

…but what could we do?

For the few folks that came to the Greenhouse that morning, it was a time to be angry, frustrated and sad. Emotions ran high, the feelings deserved airtime & space.

After some time venting, we started to rally. What could we do? Life gave us 🍋s, couldn’t we engineer some lemonade?

Posters are Important

Everything we do, has to have some style. The inverse of Gargage in, Garbage Out. Matt Brucker jumped on the “campaign rollout”. How were we gonna get the word out in style?

An idea emerged. At Olin, the networked printers have the ability to email scans/copies to folks. Apparently, students had taken to scanning hand-written notes on printers and having those printers email “all-students@olin.edu”. Anonymity via email isn’t easy, this was a clever workaround folks had figured out lately.

Buy-in from students is very different, and a lot harder, than Faculty or Staff. integrity of the student communication channel is.

Real Homemade, by Everyone

This was a Lo-fi, Hi-Fi gig. Homemade, but real.

Though Matt designed the “Diplomas” in word in about 15minutes, they had integrity. The .doc was sent along to Registrars’ Office, who then ran a “mail merge” to sync all of the graduates names of the .doc followed the regular production process of real diplomas.
