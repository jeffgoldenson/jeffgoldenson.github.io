a blueprint for temporary public gathering

If you wanted to be with a group of people in a safe, sheltered environment, where would you go right now (Fall 2020)?

Our buildings were never designed to satisfy an airborne pandemic. Our rooms are too small, HVAC systems too weak, circulation corridors too narrow. Amidst the cloud of Covid-19, how might we create temporary spaces for socially-distanced gathering?

We need each other’s company, perhaps now more than ever.

Design Criteria:

  • Safe: Fire Code & Building Code compliant
  • Cheap - both in initial cost and run costs
  • Fast - quick set-up and takedown
  • Minimally wasteful - consideration of waste and re-use are essential
  • Flexible - we’re piloting a space without a use, any successful design must accommodate needs as they emerge
What follows is an inquiry into an approximately 40’ by 40’ shelter that could accommodate approximately 16 - 24 individuals spaced more than 6’ apart.

The two most inexpensive and available pop-up shelters are tents and scaffolding.

Scaffolding

While the learning curve is steeper and solutions less turn-key, scaffolding meets the criteria in a more affordable and flexible format. One still must find a third party can handle roofing and weights/staking, which is tricky but manageable.

Power - commercial extension cords or even conduit terminating in outlets or power cords is an easy solution/
