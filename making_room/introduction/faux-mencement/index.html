How to “Graduate” in 26 Hours

When we came together Wednesday, March 11th, 9:15 for final get together before Spring Break, everything was different.

Not long after, this note went to “Carpe” one of the pillars of Olin email list life.

Everyone at the table

Welcome to the Greenhouse🌱.

We’re a mostly no-credit, non-departmental creative studio inside Olin College. We grow young ideas, not plants.

We’re a crosscutting team - staff, faculty and students alike - sitting around a table making time for 🧠storming together. We’re present for each other as people. And sometimes the personal connections across barriers and roles that only shared failure can cement, magic happens in the round.

For the few folks that came to the Greenhouse that morning, it was a time to be angry, frustrated and sad. Emotions ran high, the feelings deserved airtime & space.

…but what could we do?

After some time venting, we rallied. What could we do? Life gave us 🍋s, couldn’t we engineer some lemonade?

Promote It

Everything we do, has to have some style. The inverse of Gargage in, Garbage Out. Matt Brucker jumped on the “campaign rollout”. How were we gonna get the word out in style?

An idea emerged. At Olin, the networked printers have the ability to email scans/copies to folks. Apparently, students had taken to scanning hand-written notes on printers and having those printers email “all-students@olin.edu”. Anonymity via email isn’t easy, this was a clever workaround folks had figured out lately.

Buy-in from students is very different, and a lot harder, than Faculty or Staff. Integrity of the student communication channel/campaign is critical and their’s alone to handle.

Make it

This was a Lo-fi, all hands operation.

Mortar boards were folded en-masse that night at SLAC (Stay Late and Create) in the Library.

But Faux-mencement was real, down to the diplomas. Matt designed the “Diplomas” in MS-word in about 15 minutes, handed it off to Susan. Susan sent it along to Registrars’ Office, where Vivian ran a “mail merge” to sync graduate names with (almost earned) degrees. This was the traditional non-corona process, carrying the imprimatur of our Registrar, Linda - except it read “☻ congratulætions Olin student💥…”
