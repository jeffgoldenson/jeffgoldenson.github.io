Lita Judge’s, Pennies for Elephants , 2009 The book opens with a paperboy shouting “Pennies for elephants!” Henry and Dorothy, our two kid guides through this adventure stop to read the Boston Post article.

THREE TRAINED PACHYDERMS FOR SALE!

Mr. and Mrs. William Orford, noted animal trainers, are retiring from show business. City can’t afford to buy the elephants for the zoo, but Orfords agree to give the children of Boston two months to college $6,000. Just imagine, kids — you could ride elephants at the zoo someday!

What follows is their efforts, and the kid’s of the whole northeast’s efforts, to raise the money to bring the elephants Mollie, Tony, and Waddy in their zoo.

Here is the author, Lita Judge’s reflections from the final two pages of the book.

Author Bases Story on True Events

What unfolds is a story based on the 1914 newspaper headlines about the children of Boston raising more than $6000 to buy the first elephants fr the Franklin Park Zoo. The zoo had twenty bears, three elk, a deer, and a monkey, but no elephants. During a time when ethnicity and social class divided people, this campaign to support the elephant fund, by kids and for kids, prodded a cause which united the city. The (Boston) Post’s newly appointed “elephant editor” ran strifes every day from March to June. Every donation, from one cent up, was acknowledged in a daily list of contributors. The author was intrigued by how the elephant fund campaign inspired children to work together for a community cause.

Dynamics

There are some social dynamics in operation behind this story that make it particularly inspirational.

  • Kids-only : a fundraising challenge is issued to kids only from adults
  • Desirable goal (elephants!): that appeals to a cross-section of kids
  • Timeline: two months
  • Competition: “LONDON ZOO OFFERS $6,000 ON THE SPOT FOR TRAINED ELEPHANTS”

Campaign Progress

  • Daily Updates: (“$.14 from Roger for washing 14 windows”) in print in the Post for everyone to see
  • Business involvement: movie theaters we’re hosting showing where proceeds went to elephants.
  • A Welcome Home: a city-wide parade and celebration at Fenway park in honor of the elephants.

Notable Outputs

  • Kids work to raise money
  • Kids produce experiences to raise money — pirate party fundraiser!
  • Cause-driven action at a city-scale
